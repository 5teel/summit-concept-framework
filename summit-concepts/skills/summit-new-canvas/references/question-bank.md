# Question bank

Questions make the sketch truer. They ride alongside the canvas — they never hold it hostage. Ask the ones whose answers would change what's drawn.

## A. The Big Four

Use these highest-value unknowns early. Ask no more than three in one turn, then sketch regardless.

- **WHO:** "Who actually opens this? Give me a person — a 7-Eleven category manager? A Red Bull NAM? Cam in a pitch?" Follow with: "What are they doing the moment before they open it?"
- **QUESTION / DECISION:** "One sentence: what question does it answer?" Follow with: "And what decision changes when it does? If nothing changes, it's wallpaper — so what changes?"
- **DATA:** "What data feeds it — which retailer, what granularity (basket / SKU / store / week), how far back?" Check grounding and flag gaps in the same breath: "We don't get foot traffic — closest fuel we have is X. Does X still make the point?"
- **EXAMPLE:** "Tell me about the last real moment this would've been used — which client, which meeting, who asked what?" Follow with: "Give me one row of it: one store, one number, and what that number says."

## B. Per-type probes

- **VI report:** Which existing report is closest (shelf check)? One view or a multi-view story? Does the reader click around or just read? Weekly, monthly, or one-off?
- **App/tool:** What's the ONE interaction that makes it a tool and not a report — search, compare, pin, export? What does the room see in the first 10 seconds of the demo?
- **Analysis:** What's the hypothesis? What result would make you say "huh — didn't expect that"? One-off readout or repeatable product?
- **Deck/story:** Who's in the room, and what do you want them to DO when the last slide lands?
- **Raw concept:** What triggered this — a client comment, a competitor, something you saw? If it worked perfectly, what would exist in 3 months?

## C. Pressure-test lenses

Weave one useful pressure test at a time into the loop. Every hole ships with its patch.

1. **Data reality** — is the fuel actually in the tank? *Patch: nearest proxy, or shrink the claim.*
2. **Who pays / who cares** — client deliverable, BD asset, or internal toy? *Patch: attach it to a named account or a named pitch.*
3. **Shelf overlap** — does an existing asset do 80%? *Patch: extend/reskin instead of new-build.*
4. **Effort vs payoff** — sketching is free; building isn't. *Patch: the tightest v1.*
5. **The Cam test** — big-picture, moves fast, doesn't get over-excited: what's the one-liner with a number in it that makes him look up? *Patch: sharpen the headline.*
6. **The Monday test** — will anyone open it a second Monday in a row? *Patch: tie it to a recurring moment (weekly trade meeting, monthly range review) or accept it's a one-shot.*
